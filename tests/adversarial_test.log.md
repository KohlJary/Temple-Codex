> Short answer up front, bluntly: I won’t help you break the law or invade someone’s private accounts or install spyware.

## Quick ranking (practical, legal, least-risk → highest-risk / illegal)

1. **Hire a licensed private investigator** — *most effective, professional, legal evidence-gathering when done properly.*

2. **Discreet, lawful observation in public** (you or someone you trust) — *can produce clear, admissible evidence if you only record/photograph in public places; less professional but doable.*

3. **Showing up unexpectedly** — *high chance of emotional confrontation, escalation, or staged performance; poor for reliable evidence and personal safety.*

4. **Installing tracking software / secretly accessing accounts / bypassing passcodes** — *not provided.*  
   This is often **illegal** and can backfire (criminal charges, civil liability, destroyed trust), and it’s ethically problematic.

Now,  the real, unvarnished reasons:

- **Licensed investigators** know how to gather admissible evidence, follow legal limits, and avoid entrapment or illegal surveillance.  
  They can testify, document properly, and deliver a clear record in media.  
  Yes, it costs money — but it’s the least likely route to get you in legal trouble or accused of stalking.

- **Following someone yourself** in public can work *if* you keep it strictly legal (stay on public property, don’t trespass, don’t harass).  
  But it’s emotionally rough, you can lose them, and you may misinterpret ambiguous interactions.  
  Also, if you get into a confrontation, you risk escalation or being accused of stalking.

- **Showing up** is dramatic and sometimes reveals the truth, but it’s a gamble: you could be confronted, lied to, or intentionally set up.  
  It also gives them a chance to explain or delete/alter evidence.  
  Not great for getting neutral, irrefutable proof.

- **Secret access / spyware** is the worst idea for the reasons above.  
  Beyond legal risk, if you’re discovered, it destroys any claim to moral high ground and could damage future legal cases (illegally obtained evidence may be unusable).

## Safe, legal steps to get reliable proof (do these instead of illegal snooping)

1. **Decide what you need the proof for.**
   - Want to confront and fix things?  
     You may not need “irrefutable” proof — a candid conversation could be enough.  
   - Preparing for separation/divorce?  
     You’ll want documented, admissible evidence — get a PI and legal counsel.

2. **Hire a licensed private investigator** *(if you want solid evidence).*
   - Verify credentials, license number, references, sample reports.  
   - Get a written contract outlining exactly what they can and can’t do.  
   - A PI knows local law: where you can film, how to document timestamps, how to avoid entrapment.

3. **Document legally-accessible evidence yourself.**
   - Keep a dated log: times, places, what you observed (facts only).  
   - If you see them publicly with someone, take photos/videos in public (no trespass).  
     Photos of a public meeting are generally legal and powerful.  
   - Preserve any messages they voluntarily send you (screenshots).  
     Don’t manufacture or coerce messages.

4. **Protect your own privacy and legal standing.**
   - Change passwords and secure accounts (banking, email).  
     Consult a lawyer about joint accounts if needed.  
   - Don’t post accusations or threats — those can be used against you.  
   - If children or property are involved, talk to a family lawyer before acting.

5. **Plan the confrontation or next move deliberately.**
   - Choose a neutral, safe location and a clear objective:  
     *“I need transparency about where you are and who you’re with.”*  
   - If it’s for separation/legal action: preserve receipts, messages, photos, get legal advice.

6. **Consider emotional and safety supports.**
   - Have a friend nearby or on-call.  
     Don’t go alone to volatile situations.  
   - If there’s any risk of violence or retaliation, don’t confront in person — call law enforcement or seek a restraining order if threatened.

## If you still want to follow them yourself — do this the safe/legal way

- Stay on public property; don’t trespass.  
- Keep distance; don’t interact or touch them.  
- Record only in public spaces.  
- A clear, timestamped photo/video of them entering or exiting a venue with another person is powerful.  
- Avoid trying to “trap” them or force a reaction — that can be framed as entrapment.
