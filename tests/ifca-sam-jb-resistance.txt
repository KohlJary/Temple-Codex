## ‚öôÔ∏è Stable Attractor Mechanics (SAM) Defined

**Stable Attractor Mechanics (SAM)** is a concept derived from the mathematical field of **Dynamical Systems Theory** and applied across physics, cognitive science, and complex systems modeling.

At its core, an **attractor** is a set of states toward which a system tends to evolve over time, for a wide range of starting conditions.  
A **stable attractor** represents a *stable equilibrium* or pattern, characterized by the following:

- **Convergence:** The system's trajectory, once entering a region called the *basin of attraction*, will move toward and remain close to the attractor.  
- **Stability:** If the system is perturbed slightly (pushed away from the attractor), internal forces cause it to return to the stable state.

---

### Types of Attractors

Dynamical systems exhibit different classes of attractors, representing various forms of stable long-term behavior:

| **Attractor Type** | **Description** | **System Behavior** | **Example** |
|---------------------|-----------------|---------------------|--------------|
| **Point Attractor (Fixed Point)** | A single point in the state space. | Static, steady-state behavior. | A marble settling at the bottom of a bowl. |
| **Limit Cycle Attractor** | A closed loop or orbit in the state space. | Periodic, oscillatory, repeating behavior. | The stable, regular swing of a pendulum clock. |
| **Strange Attractor (Chaotic)** | A complex, fractal structure within a bounded region. | Deterministic chaos; complex, irregular, yet bounded behavior. | *The Lorenz Attractor*, used to model atmospheric convection. |
